Common Questions

Mobile Payment Processing FAQ

How does mobile credit card processing work without WiFi?

Mobile card readers like Clover Flex and Clover Go use 4G/LTE cellular data to process transactions. They don't require WiFi, making them perfect for outdoor events, carnivals, farmers markets, and any location where WiFi isn't available or reliable. The cellular connection is built-in to the device (Flex) or uses your phone's connection (Go).

How long does a mobile card reader battery last?

The Clover Flex runs 8+ hours on a single charge, providing enough battery life for a full day of outdoor events, food trucks, and street sales. The Clover Go pairs with your smartphone and uses your phone's battery. Both devices support offline mode, so transactions continue processing even if signal drops temporarily.

Can I use mobile card readers at multiple locations?

Yes. Your mobile card readers travel with you from location to location. Whether you're at a farmers market, pop-up retail event, food truck rotation, or traveling show, your devices are ready to accept payments immediately — no re-setup required. One account, unlimited locations.

What payment methods do mobile card readers accept?

Mobile card readers accept chip cards, swipe, contactless tap, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. This gives your customers the fastest, most convenient way to pay — and you get paid instantly. No cash-only bottlenecks, no lost sales to customers without bills.

Can I combine mobile processing with a cash discount program?

Absolutely. By offering a small cash discount (typically 3–3.99%) for customers who pay cash, you can offset 100% of your card processing fees and bring your monthly statement down to $100 or less, regardless of transaction volume. Cash discount programs are legal in all 50 states.

What happens if cellular signal drops?

Mobile card readers are offline-capable. Transactions continue processing even if signal drops temporarily — they sync when the connection returns. This is critical for outdoor events where coverage can be spotty. Your customers never experience payment delays.
